How to Tie a Tie: The Four-in-Hand Knot

Learn to tie the classic and versatile Four-in-Hand knot, one of the simplest and most popular tie knots. Perfect for beginners, this knot creates a slender, slightly asymmetrical look suitable for most occasions and collar types.

Drape and Position the Tie

‚è±Ô∏è Estimated time: 30 seconds

Stand in front of a mirror with your shirt collar turned up. Drape the tie around your neck, underneath the collar. The wide end of the tie should hang on your dominant side (e.g., your right side if you are right-handed), and the narrow end on the other. Adjust the tie so the wide end hangs about 12 inches (30 cm) lower than the narrow end. The seam of the tie should be facing your shirt.

‚è±Ô∏è Estimated time: 30 seconds

Take the wide end of the tie and cross it over the top of the narrow end, moving it towards your non-dominant side. The wide end should now be on the opposite side from where it started. Hold the crossover point with your non-dominant thumb and index finger to keep it secure against your chest.

Bring the Wide End Under

‚è±Ô∏è Estimated time: 30 seconds

Continue holding the crossover point. Now, bring the wide end of the tie underneath the narrow end, moving it back towards your dominant side. The wide end should now be back where it started, with its seam facing outwards.

4

‚è±Ô∏è Estimated time: 30 seconds

Bring the wide end back over the top of the knot one more time, moving it from your dominant to your non-dominant side. This creates the front loop of the knot you will eventually pass the tie through. The wide end should now be on your non-dominant side again, with its front side facing out.

‚è±Ô∏è Estimated time: 30 seconds

Now, pass the wide end of the tie upwards through the loop that has formed around your neck. Guide the tip of the wide end up between your collar and the tie knot, emerging from behind the knot.

6

‚è±Ô∏è Estimated time: 30 seconds

You should now see the front loop you created in Step 4. Carefully pass the wide end of the tie down through this front loop. Pull the wide end all the way through, but do not pull it tight yet.

üí° Tips:
  • Use your index finger to slightly open the front loop, making it easier to thread the wide end through without twisting it.
7

Tighten and Adjust

‚è±Ô∏è Estimated time: 1 minute

To tighten the knot, hold the narrow end of the tie with one hand and gently slide the knot up towards your collar with the other hand. Adjust until the knot is snug but not constricting. The tip of the wide end should end at or just above your belt buckle. Once satisfied, flip down your collar and center the knot.

üí° Tips:
  • To create a classic 'dimple' just below the knot, press your index finger into the center of the tie just below the knot as you perform the final tightening. Pinch the sides of the tie around your finger to set the dimple.
  • The narrow end can be tucked into the keeper loop on the back of the wide end to keep it hidden and neat.

  • Do not overtighten the tie. You should be able to fit one or two fingers comfortably between the tie and your neck.
