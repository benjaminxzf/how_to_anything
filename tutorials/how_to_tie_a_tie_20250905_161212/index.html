How to Tie a Tie: The Four-in-Hand Knot

Learn to tie the classic and versatile Four-in-Hand knot, a simple and popular choice for any occasion. This step-by-step guide will have you looking sharp in minutes.

You have now created a small horizontal loop on the front of the knot with the last crossover. Carefully feed the tip of the wide end down through this front loop. Do not pull it tight yet.

Hold the narrow end of the tie with one hand and gently pull down on the wide end with the other to tighten the knot. Once it's snug, slide the entire knot up towards your collar by holding the knot with one hand and pulling on the narrow end with the other. Adjust until the knot is centered and sits comfortably against your collar.

Final Touches

Fold your shirt collar down over the tie. Check the length: the tip of the wide end should end at or just above your belt buckle. The narrow end should be shorter and can be tucked into the keeper loop on the back of the wide end. Create a 'dimple' just below the knot by pinching the fabric before the final tightening for a polished look.
